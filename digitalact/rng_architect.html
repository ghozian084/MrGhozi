🎲 Project 3: The RNG Architect

Master the Math of Game Design.

Your task is to design a digital mini-game and prove exactly how likely a player is to win using Probability and Combinatorics.

Tools: Canva for design + Google Sheets or Calculator for math.

Step 1: Game Concept

Design a simple game mechanic using one of these tools:

  • Dice: Rolling two 6-sided dice.
  • Cards: Drawing 2 cards from a small deck of 10 unique cards.
  • Spinners: A wheel with different sized sections.

Action: Use Canva to design a quick visual of your game board or cards.

Step 2: Total Outcomes

Use the Multiplication Principle to find all possible results.

Example (2 Dice): 6 x 6 = 36 total outcomes.
Example (Cards): If you have 10 cards and draw 2, how many ways can they be arranged?
P(10, 2) = 10! / (10-2)! = 90 ways.

Step 3: Define "The Win"

Decide what counts as a "Win" in your game.

  • Example (Dice): Rolling a sum of 7 or 11.
  • Example (Cards): Drawing the "King" and "Ace" together.

Task: List every "Winning Combination" and count them ($n$).

Step 4: The Win Rate

Calculate your game's probability as a Fraction, Decimal, and Percentage.

P(Win) = (Winning Outcomes) / (Total Outcomes)

Example: 6 / 36 = 1/6 (Fraction)
1 ÷ 6 ≈ 0.166 (Decimal)
0.166 x 100 = 16.6% (Percentage)

Step 5: Game Balance

Is your game fair? Use basic logic to explain:

  • If Win Rate < 10%: The game is "Hard/Expert."
  • If Win Rate 40-50%: The game is "Fair/Casual."
  • If Win Rate > 70%: The game is "Too Easy."

Challenge: If a player plays 5 times, what is the chance they win 0 times? (Power rule: $LoseRate^5$).
