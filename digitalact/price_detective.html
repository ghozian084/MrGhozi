🔍 Project 2: The Price Detective

Can you find the hidden price of a single burger?

In this project, you will use Systems of Equations to solve real-world pricing mysteries from online stores.

Objective: Find 2 bundles, create 2 equations, and solve for 2 variables.

Step 1: The Digital Hunt

Go to a food delivery app (GoFood/Grab) or online store (Shopee/Amazon).

  • Find two different "Bundles" that contain the same items but in different quantities.
  • Example:
    Bundle A: 2 Pizzas + 1 Drink = $25
    Bundle B: 1 Pizza + 2 Drinks = $17
  • Action: Take screenshots of both bundles.

Step 2: Algebraic Modeling

Assign variables to your items and write the equations.

Let x = Price of Item 1 (Pizza)
Let y = Price of Item 2 (Drink)

Eq 1: 2x + 1y = 25
Eq 2: 1x + 2y = 17

Make sure your prices include the decimals (e.g., $24.99).

Step 3: Solving the Mystery

Use the Elimination or Substitution method to find x and y.

  • Show your step-by-step work.
  • If there is a discount (e.g., 10% off), represent it as a decimal (0.90) in your equation.
(Solve for x and y here)

Step 4: The Comparison

Now, find the Individual Price of one item if bought alone (not in a bundle).

  • Is the bundle actually cheaper?
  • Calculate the Percentage Saved using the formula:
% Saved = [(Individual Price - Bundle Price) / Individual Price] x 100
