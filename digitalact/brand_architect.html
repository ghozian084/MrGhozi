📦 Project 4: The 3D Brand Architect

Geometry in the Palm of Your Hand.

Your task is to design a digital 3D package for a product and calculate the exact math needed to manufacture it.

Tools: Canva (3D Elements) or Tinkercad + Google Slides.

Step 1: Product Concept

Pick a product to design a box for (e.g., a new smartphone, a luxury perfume, or a futuristic snack).

  • Decide on the shape: Rectangular Prism (box) or Cylinder (tube).
  • Action: Use Canva or Tinkercad to create a 3D visual of your package.

Step 2: Dimensions & Decimals

Assign realistic dimensions (Length, Width, Height) or (Radius, Height).

Use Decimals for precision (e.g., 15.5 cm).

Example:
Length (l) = 12.4 cm
Width (w) = 6.2 cm
Height (h) = 2.5 cm

Step 3: Surface Area (The Shell)

Calculate the total area of the "Net" (all sides combined). This tells you how much cardboard/material you need.

Formula (Box): 2(lw + lh + wh)

Calculation:
2( (12.4*6.2) + (12.4*2.5) + (6.2*2.5) ) = [___] cm²

Step 4: Volume (The Capacity)

Calculate how much space is inside the package.

Formula (Box): l x w x h
Formula (Cylinder): π x r² x h

Calculation: 12.4 x 6.2 x 2.5 = [___] cm³

Step 5: The "Mini" Version (Scaling)

Imagine you make a "Travel Size" version that is exactly 1/2 the size ($0.5x$).

  • What happens to the Volume? (Hint: It's not just 1/2!)
  • New Volume = Original Volume x ($0.5^3$).
Volume scaling uses Powers of 3!
