Research: Literature vs. Digital Reading

Researcher: Statistical Exemplar Group

Hypothesis

We predict that students who own more physical books (Discrete) will have a higher daily reading duration (Continuous), but with high variation (Standard Deviation).

Cambridge P1 Topics Covered:
- Representation of Data (1.1 - 1.5)
- Measures of Central Tendency (2.1 - 2.3)
- Measures of Variation (3.1 - 3.3)

1. Data Classification (Topic 1.1)

Variable A: Books Owned

Type: Discrete

Reason: You cannot own 12.4 books. Values are restricted to whole integers (counts).

Variable B: Reading Time

Type: Continuous

Reason: Time can be measured to any degree of accuracy (e.g., 42.56 minutes).

Manual Calculation Proof for σ:
Using formula: √[ (Σx² / n) - (x̄)² ]
For books: √[ (13200 / 20) - (25.1)² ] = √[ 660 - 630.01 ] = √29.99 = 5.47

6. Research Conclusion

Our research found a moderate positive correlation between books owned and reading time. However, the Standard Deviation for reading time was very high (31.4), indicating that "Time Spent" is highly inconsistent among students compared to "Books Owned."

Skewness: The reading time data showed a positive skew, as a small number of students read for over 120 minutes, pulling the mean higher than the median.
