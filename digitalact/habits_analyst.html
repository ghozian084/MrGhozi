📊 Project 5: The Digital Habits Analyst

Turn your phone usage into Data Science.

In this final project, you will analyze your own Screen Time data to find patterns in your digital life using Statistics.

Objective: Collect 7 days of data, calculate the Mean, Median, Mode, and create a Chart.

Step 1: Data Harvesting

Go to your phone settings (Screen Time on iPhone or Digital Wellbeing on Android).

  • Action: Take a screenshot of your usage for the last 7 days.
  • List the total minutes/hours used each day (e.g., Mon: 4h 20m, Tue: 3h 15m, etc.).
  • Convert everything into Minutes for easier math.
Example: 4h 20m = (4 x 60) + 20 = 260 Minutes.

Step 2: The Central Tendency

Calculate the 3 Ms of Statistics:

  • Mean (Average): Total Minutes ÷ 7 Days.
  • Median (Middle): Sort your 7 days from smallest to largest. Pick the middle one.
  • Mode: The app category you used most frequently (e.g., Social, Games, Education).
Example: Data: 120, 150, 150, 180, 200, 210, 300
Median = 180 Minutes.

Step 3: The Range (Dispersion)

How much does your usage fluctuate?

Formula: Highest Value - Lowest Value = Range.

Range = 300 mins - 120 mins = 180 Minutes.

A large range means your habits are inconsistent. A small range means your habits are very stable.

Step 4: Data Visualization

Create a Bar Chart or Pie Chart using Google Sheets or Canva.

  • X-Axis: Days of the week.
  • Y-Axis: Minutes used.
  • Make it colorful! Visuals help people understand data instantly.
